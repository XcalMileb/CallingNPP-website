    ABOUT LAOS

    Country Facts

    Laos, officially the Lao People's Democratic Republic (LPDR), is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia. It is bordered by Myanmar and China to the northwest, Vietnam to the east, Cambodia to the southeast, and Thailand to the west and southwest. Its capital and most populous city is Vientiane(万象市).

    Area: 236 800 km²

    Population: 7 million

    Geography

    Laos is the only landlocked country in Southeast Asia, and it lies mostly between latitudes 14° and 23°N (an area is south of 14°), and longitudes 100° and 108°E. Its forested landscape consists mostly of mountains, the highest of which is Phou Bia at 2,818 metres (9,245 ft), with some plains and plateaus.

    Why You Should Travel to Laos

      Laos, often called the "Land of a Million Elephants," is a hidden gem in Southeast Asia that captivates travelers with its natural beauty, rich culture, and serene ambiance. Whether you're a seasoned adventurer or a first-time explorer, Laos offers a unique and unforgettable experience.

    1. Natural Beauty: Discover Kuang Si Waterfalls, Mekong River cruises, and karst mountains in Vang Vieng.

    2. Tranquil Atmosphere: Escape the hustle and enjoy Laos’ peaceful, laid-back lifestyle.

    3. Cultural Heritage: Visit UNESCO-listed Luang Prabang, ancient temples, and experience traditional almsgiving.

    4. Authentic Experiences: Explore rural villages, traditional crafts, and unique Lao cuisine like larb and sticky rice.

    5. Affordable Adventure: Trekking, caving, and scenic boat rides make Laos a budget-friendly paradise.

      With warm locals, untouched nature, and vibrant traditions, Laos is the perfect getaway for culture and adventure seekers alike.
